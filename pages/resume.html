About Me

I discovered purely functional programming in my late teens and later became a zealot. For me all computation was sum types and pattern matching. I used to consider myself an amateur compiler engineer (though not in a professional capacity) and by this I mean parsing, data modeling and AST analysis and transformation including Hindley-Milner type inference. This is what taught me how to solve hard abstract problems at an early age. In some ways, I became a very nonstandard individual with a unique way of seeing things. Spent most of my twenties trying to do my own thing from which I’ve learned much when it comes to solving hard and very challenging problems except making money.


As a programmer I’m especially skilled when it comes to problems in the world of Rust & Swift including their associated ecosystems, and solving the kinda problems that chatbots cannot solve such as custom TextKit2 rendering.
